Teacher salaries can't keep up in the Silicon Valley
By Joaquin Palomino and Heather Knight
April 15, 2015
The pay gap between the sexes in San Francisco has been wide at times in the past. But in 2009, as the recession was winding down, fully employed men and women in the city had roughly the same median income: $66,000 and $64,000 per year, respectively, after adjusting for inflation. By 2014, men working full time made about $73,400 per year, $11,300 more than women on average.
